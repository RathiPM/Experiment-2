# Experiment-2 India’s Top 5 Hindu Temples

"India’s Top 5 Hindu Temples "

Famous Temples

  • Meenakshi Amman Temple
  • Virupaksha Temple
  • Khajuraho Group of Temples
  • Brihadeeswarar Temple
  • Puri Jagannath Temple

  • Meenakshi Amman Temple

    • Meenakshi Amman Temple is dedicated t goddess Meenakshi Amman and Sundareswar(a form of Lord Shiva and lord Parvathi). This temple is found in literature that was written in 7th century and thus, it is believed that it was built before that period. The present structure was built in 17th century as a remodeling due to damage by invaders. This temple has 14 towers and 2 golden gopuras. This temple has a hall of thousand pillars. Every year, millions of people visit this temple.This temple is located in Madurai,Tamil Nadu.

  • Virupaksha Temple

    • Virupaksha Temple is the oldest Hindu temple that is still functioning from the day of inauguration. It was built in 7th century. It is not just a simple temple. It is a complex of many temples and most of them were built during Vijayaraja dynasty. This temple is located in Hampi which is 350 km away from Bangalore, Karnataka. It is a world heritage site. This temple has pillared halls, gateway and courtyard. The gateway is a very majestic one which stands, 500 metre tall.

  • Khajuraho Group of Temples

    • Khajuraho group of temples is a world heritage site and the only temple in India with erotic sculptures. These temples were built in 9th and 10th century. A few temples in this complex are dedicated to Jainism. This complex is also famous for temples for Brahma, creator of world. These temples are located about 175 km away from Jhansi, Madhya Pradesh, India. A lot of people visit here for its collection of extraordinary sculptures.

  • Brihadeeswarar Temple

    • Brihadeeswarar Temple is located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. This shrine is dedicated to Lord Shiva and Parvathi. It was built in 10th century and is the first temple in India to be built completely with granite. It is one of the world heritage sites and is also called as the Great Living Chola Temple. This is world’s tallest temple tower of 216 ft height. The apex of this temple is a single rock that weighs 80 ton. This is an important tourist destination in south India. The temple car festival is an important event in this temple.

  • Puri Jagannath Temple

    • The main deity is made of wood and is replaced at intervals. This temple was built in 12th century. It is said that this temple was built on the place of ruins of an ancient temple. This temple has 21 shrines in it. The chariot wheel in this temple is an important element. A lot of people visit here during the car festival. It is one of the few temples which should be visited by Hindus at least once in his lifetime. It is a heritage site and is completely made of granite stone.

  • YOUTAN POLUO

    • "Youtanpoluo" refers to a phenomenon where certain types of Buddhist temple decorations appear to transform into tiny, bead-like structures that resemble small pearls or berries. This phenomenon has been associated with certain artworks and decorations in temples, particularly in East Asia. In reality, Youtanpoluo are not actual plant seeds or pearls, but rather a type of fungal growth that can occur on wooden surfaces or organic materials.

  • GIBRALTAR CAMPION

    • The Gibraltar Campion is a critically endangered flowering plant that is endemic to the British Overseas Territory of Gibraltar. This plant is known for its silvery-gray leaves and small, delicate white flowers. It was once believed to be extinct, but a few individuals were rediscovered on the rocky cliffs of Gibraltar in the 1990s. Conservation efforts have been made to protect and propagate this rare plant species.

  • CHOCALATE COSMOS

    • The Chocolate Cosmos is a unique and captivating flowering plant known for its deep, rich, chocolate-like fragrance. Native to Mexico, it produces dark red to nearly black velvety flowers that resemble cosmos flowers in shape. Despite its name, the Chocolate Cosmos doesn't actually taste like chocolate.The plant was thought to be extinct in its native habitat, but it was later reintroduced through cultivation.
